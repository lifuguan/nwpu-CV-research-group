About IAPR (International Association for Pattern Recognition)

The International Association for Pattern Recognition (IAPR) was founded in 1978 and is composed of experts and organizations in the field of pattern recognition from around the world.

IAPR is one of the most authoritative international academic organizations in the field of pattern recognition and is a member of the International Federation for Information Processing (IFIP).

Official website: https://iapr.org/

About IAPR Fellow

The IAPR Fellow honor was established in 1994 to recognize outstanding researchers and activity organizers in the field of pattern recognition.

üéØ Key Facts about IAPR Fellow

Established: 1994

Frequency: Elected once every two years

Purpose: To honor outstanding scientific researchers and activity organizers in the field of pattern recognition

Nomination: Must be nominated by an existing Fellow

Selection Rate: No more than 0.25% (2.5 per thousand) of total membership

2022 Selection: Only 21 people worldwide were elected as IAPR Fellow

Rigorous Selection Process

The selection process for IAPR Fellow is extremely rigorous and selective:

  • Nomination Requirement: Candidates must be nominated by an already-elected IAPR Fellow
  • Strict Quota: The election rate cannot exceed 0.25% of the total membership, making it one of the most exclusive honors in pattern recognition
  • Biennial Selection: Elections are held only once every two years, ensuring only the most distinguished candidates are considered
  • Global Competition: In 2022, out of thousands of IAPR members worldwide, only 21 individuals received this prestigious honor
